## HSS-side observation metaphor

### Kasuru

Use:
In this report, `kasuru` is treated not as a strict external technical term, but as an HSS-side observation metaphor.

Here, `kasuru` refers to a state in which a symbol does not enter the center of the receiver’s reconnectable area, but touches its edge and remains as unease, memory, fragment, or trace.

## HSS-side use

In Report 004, HSS does not evaluate whether a word, expression, culture, buzz, meme, innovation, or receiver reaction is good or bad.

Instead, it observes a connection structure in which `sasaru` and `kasuru` differ by how a symbol connects to the receiver’s layered history and reconnectable area.

The source anchors are used to confirm that metaphorical uses of `sasaru`, cultural propagation, memes, internet memes, viral spread, and diffusion are externally recognizable contexts.

They are not used to prove HSS.

The report observes `sasaru` as a state in which a symbol connects strongly to an existing layered history or reconnectable area.

It observes `kasuru` as a state in which a symbol does not fully connect, but leaves a trace that may remain and return later as a future connection candidate.
