This note does not provide an exhaustive summary of the sources.

The sources listed here are used to confirm the circulated contexts around one-shot gags, buzz, propagation, memes, and performance systems.

They are not HSS foundations and are not used as HSS evidence.

## HSS-side use

In Report 002, HSS does not evaluate whether a specific gag, performer, meme, or viral phenomenon is good or bad.

Instead, it observes a connection structure in which a strong short symbol may either connect to an accumulated performance system or circulate only as short-cycle buzz.

The source anchors are used to confirm that one-shot gags, owarai terminology, buzz, viral propagation, word-of-mouth circulation, and memes are externally recognizable contexts.

They are not used to prove HSS.

The report observes the difference between a case where a one-shot symbol connects to a performance system and a case where the processing form circulates without propagating connection possibility.

It also observes how a strong short symbol can be consumed, repeated, imitated, and circulated without necessarily reconnecting to layered performance history.

## Notes

These sources are used as source anchors.

They are not HSS foundations, HSS evidence, or HSS authorities.

They are used to keep the external context visible before applying HSS as a tentative observation axis.
