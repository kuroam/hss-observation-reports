# 003. Innovation / Business Process Sources

This source note supports `reports/ja/003_innovation_invoice_bundle.md`.

This note does not provide an exhaustive summary of the sources.

It provides source anchors for the context used in the observation report.

This is not a comprehensive bibliography.

It is also not a rigorous literature review of management studies, organization theory, or innovation studies.

The sources listed here are used to confirm the circulated contexts around innovation, business cases, business processes, phase-gate processes, and formal organizations.

They are not HSS foundations and are not used as HSS evidence.

They are also not used to evaluate any specific company, organization, approval process, budget process, business case, KPI, or phase-gate process.

## HSS-side use

In Report 003, HSS does not evaluate whether a specific innovation process, approval process, budget process, business case, KPI, or phase-gate process is good or bad.

Instead, it observes a connection structure in which new connection candidates may be compressed into existing approval, budget, evaluation, and organizational processing forms.

The source anchors are used to confirm that innovation, innovation management, diffusion, business cases, business processes, phase-gate processes, and formal organizations are externally recognizable contexts.

They are not used to prove HSS.

The report observes how unprocessed candidates such as discomfort, observations, prototypes, customer reactions, and technical possibilities may either gather around a new pin or be reshaped into forms that fit existing processing pins.

In this observation, the new pin itself does not become an invoice bundle.

Rather, candidates around the new pin may be processed as an invoice bundle when they are reshaped into formats that existing organizational processing forms can handle.

## Notes

They are used to keep the external context visible before applying HSS as a tentative observation axis.
